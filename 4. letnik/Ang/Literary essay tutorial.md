2. Body - 2 to 3 paragraphs of yap connected to thesis (yoou won't have time for more) You want to prove the thesis. Then you either do option 1: you mention 2 or 3 examples from the novel and give evidence or option 2: You describe the development od the topic throughout the novel and give examples, but do not go into details. Every paragraph should have a toppic sentence so the reader knows what the paragraph will write about. Do not retell the plot. Your own commentaries = your interpretation/explanation of the concrete example
   Do not start a sentence with "I think will was immature" or "in my opinion..." instead you can say "Wills immaturity shows in bla bla". IT IS ASSUMED THAT WHAT YOU ARE WRITING IS YOUR POV - you don't have to constantly emphasize that this is how you see it and you are not trying to put words in someone's mouth. 
3. Ending - sum everything up, restate your thesis, close out in a final profound thought. In the conclusion you can also take a stance.

# Discuss Will's attitude to Marcus in the book About A Boy

Will’s attitude to Marcus in the book About a Boy by Nick Hornby reveals how disinterest can slowly transform into responsibility, even when responsibility is the last thing a person desires. Wills behavior toward Marcus exposes the tension between a way of self-preservation and genuine connection, showing that growth often begins in the moments we least expect it. Will’s evolving stance does not simply reflect his changing opinion on Marcus, it exposes how fragile his carefully constructed independence really is.

Will’s initial attitude toward Marcus is shaped by avoidance and discomfort. Marcus disrupts the predictable, detached lifestyle Will has built around himself, and Will treats him as an unwelcome complication. His dismissive reactions, such as trying to distance himself from Marcus’s problems or hoping Marcus will stop seeking him out, reveal how deeply Will fears any demand that threatens his autonomy. This early resistance highlights the extent to which Will relies on emotional distance to maintain the illusion of control.

However, Will’s attitude shifts as Marcus’s persistence forces him to confront the emptiness of his isolation. His reluctant involvement gradually turns into genuine concern, which can be seen in moments when he supports Marcus despite the inconvenience or discomfort it causes him. Will’s decisions to help Marcus at crucial points demonstrate that his detachment was never as impenetrable as he pretended. These actions show the development of an unexpected bond, one that challenges Will’s belief that relationships only lead to being caught in a situation that limits your freedom and is hard to escape..

By the end of the novel, Will’s attitude toward Marcus shows a recognition that connection is not a threat but a source of meaning. His transformation, from avoidance to engagement, reinforces the idea that responsibility becomes liberating when it is chosen rather than forced. Will’s evolving story proves that personal freedom is not taken away by caring for others. Instead, it is strengthened by the confidence that relationships are not a burden but an opportunities for connection and growth.

In the end, Will’s involvement Marcus marks a clear turning point in his emotional development. What begins as distance and avoidance becomes genuine care, showing that he no longer feels like he needs to protect himself from the real world interactions, because he was able to see that engaging with others does not weaken his independence but gives his life greater purpose .
